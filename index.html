How to design your own bunch of flowers?

Read time: 5 minutes

A beautiful bunch of flowers, looks effortless, but takes some planning! In this article we'll talk through the basics of flower arranging so that you can select your very own flowers and start building your own perfect bunch as a treat to yourself or thoughtful gift to another!

"Flowers don't tell, they show!"- Stephanie Skeem

3 tips for picking flowers for a bouquet?

  1. Stick to a color scheme: Choose flowers that incorporate similar or complementary colors to where the bouquet will live. Also, make sure that the colors within the bouquet complement each other.
  2. Check the seasons: In-season flowers are fresher and more affordable than off-season flowers, so you may want to consider choosing flowers for your bouquet that are in season.
  3. Choose hearty flowers: To make sure that your flowers look fresh as possible for as long as possible, use hearty flowers like daisies, succulents, and chrysanthemums.

Tips for preparing your bouquet:

  1. Remove any leaves and thorns
  2. Start with a focal flower and build around it
  3. Add filler flowers- smaller flowers and greenery are perfect for complementing your flowers, bulking up the bunch and adding a natural aesthetic. These could include eucalyptus, myrtle and baby's breath.
  4. Wrap the stems firmly together
  5. Add any finishing touches- a bit of ribbon always makes them look special!
